\section{V. Conclusions and Future Work}

This study highlighted the adaptive capabilities of a coalition-based mission replanning framework (MRCF) for multi-UAV patrols. Quantitative analyses demonstrated that, even under simplified UAV dynamics (2-DoF instead of the full 6-DoF of the Skywalker glider) and without realistic PID control or route optimizers, the MRCF effectively redistributed tasks among UAVs and maintained continuous coverage. The results indicate that having a centralized knowledge of all UAV states within the MAS enables better reallocation and mission execution compared to independent UAV operations. 

Despite the promising performance observed in the simulations, several limitations were noted. Simplifications in UAV dynamics, ignored aerodynamic constraints, and conceptual deviations from actual flight doctrine may impact the realism of the results. Nonetheless, the focus of this study was to evaluate whether the MRCF could self-adjust and improve mission performance through coalition-based replanning, which was successfully demonstrated by the quantitative metrics.

For future work, the MRCF will be disseminated to the academic community along with a detailed diagram of all implementable components. Efforts will also focus on enhancing simulator fidelity by integrating real modules beyond the current MRCF implementation. Additionally, research will continue with the development of the Mission Integrity and Replanning Framework (MIRF), a fault-handling module designed to address conflicts and enhance BVLOS safety under UTM regulations. This will involve integrating techniques from deep learning, knowledge networks, and collaborative decision-making, opening new possibilities for academic participation and operational validation.
